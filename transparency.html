THE PIONEER FOREST

Transparency & Scope

Structure, boundaries, and what this is — and isn’t.

The Pioneer Forest is a private, non-commercial initiative focused on Pi-powered reforestation through documented, traceable contributions.

Nature of Participation

Participation is voluntary. Contributions are non-refundable and may be used to acquire reforestation credits or plantings through third-party platforms.

Outcomes (trees, certificates, CO₂ figures) depend on external availability, project pricing, and timing of purchases. Records are published to keep the process transparent and auditable.

How Records Work

This website presents structured snapshots — impact totals, project links, and artifacts — based on documented contributions and verified planting records.

The goal is clarity: not promises, not projections — but a traceable record of what happened and what each contribution supported.

Public Record

Official announcements, ledger entries, and structural updates are published in The Pioneer Forest channel on Fireside.

This channel serves as the public announcement layer of the project. It complements this website by recording milestones in an open forum.
